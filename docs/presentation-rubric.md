Please use the presentation to demonstrate your depth of understanding. In particular, be prepared to explain why you chose your method, how it behaved on your dataset, what worked, what did not work, and what conclusions you can reasonably draw from your experiments. Briefly discussing limitations, challenges, or next steps is encouraged.

You do not need to spend much time giving textbook-level definitions of algorithms covered in class. Instead, focus on your project decisions, experiments, findings, and insights.

Please also make sure that your slides are clear, readable, and well organized so that the main motivation, methods, results, and conclusions can be understood within the time limit.

Final Project Presentation Rubric (100 points)
1. Problem framing and motivation (10 point)
Clearly explains the problem being addressed, the dataset used, and why the task is meaningful or interesting.

2. Dataset-specific understanding and preprocessing (15 points)
Clearly explains the dataset, important preprocessing steps, feature choices, and any data-related challenges relevant to the project.

3. Method choice and justification (15 points)
Clearly explains the machine learning method(s) used and, more importantly, why those methods were appropriate for this specific dataset and task.

4. Evaluation design and comparisons (15 points)
Clearly explains how performance was evaluated, including metrics, train/test setup or validation strategy, and any comparisons to baselines, alternative methods, or alternative settings.

5. Results and interpretation (20 points)
Presents results clearly and correctly. Interprets what the results mean, explains what worked and what did not, and draws reasonable conclusions from the experiments.

6. Depth of understanding (15 points)
Demonstrates real ownership of the project. Shows clear understanding of design choices, implementation decisions, limitations, and tradeoffs. Can explain how the method behaved on this dataset rather than giving only generic textbook descriptions.

7. Presentation clarity and organization (5 points)
Presentation is clear, well organized, readable, and fits within the time limit.

8. Team participation and individual contribution (5 points)
All team members participate meaningfully and are able to explain the part of the project they primarily worked on.
